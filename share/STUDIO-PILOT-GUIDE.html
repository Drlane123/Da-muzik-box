StudioPilot ≠ Da Music Box. They are two different products. StudioPilot is an Ableton Live AI control kit (studiopilotmusic.com). Da Music Box is the standalone music production suite app (Beat Lab, SE2, Mastering Bay, etc.).

What StudioPilot is

StudioPilot is a producer workflow box that sits around Ableton Live. You talk to it in plain language — it turns requests into 200+ real Ableton actions: drums, basslines, MIDI clips, scenes, locators, mix moves, and master passes.

AI can't hear your music. StudioPilot's built-in analyser reads waveforms and spectrograms so the assistant can spot clashes, find key, score your own samples, and guide mix and master decisions visually.

Producer types plain language ↓ StudioPilot (Claude or Codex / ChatGPT) ↓ 200+ Ableton actions — clips, tracks, MIDI, devices, arrangement, mix, master ↓ Finished track inside YOUR session, YOUR sounds

What it does

Create

  • Drum patterns, basslines, MIDI clips, scenes
  • Starting points without menu-diving
  • “Make a garage drum loop” · “Write a bassline in F minor”

Edit

  • Quantize, humanize velocity, transpose MIDI
  • Clip gain, audio pitch, warp markers

Arrange

  • Turn loops into song sections
  • Locators, intros, builds, drops, breakdowns
  • Duplicate clips to arrangement, loop points

Analyze

  • Waveform + spectrogram of YOUR loops
  • Band energy, brightness, rhythmic density
  • Key detection · anti-clash scoring for your library

Mix

  • Balance, pan, sends to reverb/delay
  • High-pass tops, side-chain bass to kick

Master

  • Master bus: EQ, glue comp, limiter
  • Target LUFS · match reference track
  • Analyze exported stems and tighten final file

The special ingredient — AI sees your sound

Most AI tools generate generic audio. StudioPilot reads what's already in your session:

Rhythmic densityLayer sparse parts over busy ones without collision

Example: “Best match: Percussion 10 sits in the open mid pocket — can't fight your kick, bass, or hats.”

Example command

What a producer types:

“Make a tight 8-bar house idea, create drums and bass, add a breakdown locator, and clean up the timing.”

  1. Creates MIDI clips and names the tracks
  2. Adds notes, timing, velocity, and loop lengths
  3. Sets tempo, locators, scenes, and arrangement points
  4. Adjusts clips, devices, sends, and mix parameters
